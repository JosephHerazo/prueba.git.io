General description


Donating blood is a voluntary procedure that can help save lives. There are several types of blood donation. Each type helps meet different medical needs.


Blood donation


Whole blood donation is the most common type of blood donation. During this donation, you donate about a pint (about half a liter) of whole blood. The blood is then separated into its components: red blood cells, plasma, and sometimes platelets.


Aferesis


During apheresis, you will be connected to a machine that collects and separates different elements from the blood. These blood components include red blood cells, platelets, and plasma. The machine then returns the remaining elements of the blood.
